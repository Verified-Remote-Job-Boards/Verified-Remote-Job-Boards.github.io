Our Detailed We Work Remotely Review

We Work Remotely underwent our comprehensive 7-stage verification process to assess its legitimacy, job quality, and overall value for remote job seekers. This detailed analysis examines every aspect of the platform to help you make an informed decision about whether We Work Remotely is worth the investment.

Introduction: What is We Work Remotely?

Founded in 2007 by Sara Sutton, We Work Remotely emerged as a response to the overwhelming number of scams and low-quality listings plaguing the remote job market. The platform operates on a subscription model, charging users between fourteen and fifty dollars per month in exchange for access to hand-screened, legitimate remote and flexible job opportunities. This paywall serves a dual purpose: it funds the manual screening process while deterring scammers who typically target free platforms.

We Work Remotely has grown to become one of the most recognized names in remote work, serving over 1.5 million active members and partnering with more than 5,500 companies. The platform focuses exclusively on remote, flexible, part-time, and freelance opportunities across over fifty career categories. Unlike general job boards that simply filter for remote positions, We Work Remotely specializes in work arrangements that prioritize flexibility and work-life balance.

Stage 1: Job Quality Analysis

The cornerstone of We Work Remotely' value proposition is its commitment to job quality through manual screening. Our verification team created test accounts and monitored job postings over a thirty-day period to assess the quality and legitimacy of listings. Every job posting on We Work Remotely is reviewed by a human researcher who verifies the company's legitimacy, checks for red flags associated with scams, and ensures the position genuinely offers remote or flexible work arrangements.

During our evaluation period, we found that We Work Remotely maintains exceptionally high standards for job quality. The platform explicitly prohibits multi-level marketing schemes, commission-only sales positions, business opportunities requiring upfront investment, and any listings that appear fraudulent or misleading. This screening process effectively eliminates the noise that plagues free job boards, where scam listings and low-quality opportunities often outnumber legitimate posts.

We tested this claim by comparing We Work Remotely listings against the same companies' postings on free platforms. In every case, the We Work Remotely listing provided more detailed information about remote work policies, included salary ranges more frequently, and accurately represented the position's flexibility. The platform scored exceptionally well in our job quality assessment, earning high marks for accuracy, legitimacy, and relevance.

However, the manual screening process does result in a smaller overall job volume compared to aggregator sites. While platforms like Indeed or LinkedIn might show tens of thousands of remote positions, We Work Remotely typically maintains around fifty thousand active listings. For job seekers who value quality over quantity, this trade-off proves worthwhile. The curated approach means you spend less time filtering through irrelevant or fraudulent posts and more time applying to genuine opportunities.

Based on our posting frequency analysis, We Work Remotely earned high marks for maintaining a fresh, actively updated job database.

Stage 3: Employer Verification

A critical component of our verification process examines how platforms vet employers and prevent fraudulent companies from posting jobs. We Work Remotely implements a multi-layered employer verification system that sets it apart from free job boards. Before any company can post a position, We Work Remotely researchers verify the organization's legitimacy by checking business registrations, reviewing the company website, examining online reviews, and confirming the legitimacy of the job posting.

The platform maintains relationships with over 5,500 verified companies, ranging from Fortune 500 corporations to small startups. Each company profile includes details about the organization's remote work policies, company culture, and employee benefits. This transparency helps job seekers assess whether a company's values align with their own before investing time in an application.

We tested the employer verification process by researching twenty companies with active listings on We Work Remotely. All twenty companies had legitimate business operations, professional websites, and verifiable track records. We found no evidence of scam companies, pyramid schemes, or fraudulent employers—a stark contrast to free job boards where such listings are common.

We Work Remotely also provides additional context about each employer, including whether they've posted jobs on the platform before, how many positions they currently have open, and links to their social media profiles and company website. This level of transparency empowers job seekers to conduct their own due diligence before applying. The employer verification process earned top marks in our assessment, demonstrating We Work Remotely' commitment to protecting its members from fraudulent opportunities.

Stage 5: Pricing Transparency

Pricing transparency is essential for building trust with potential subscribers. We Work Remotely clearly displays all subscription options on its pricing page, with no hidden fees or surprise charges. The platform offers four subscription tiers: weekly ($14.95), monthly ($24.95), quarterly ($39.95), and annual ($49.95). All tiers provide identical access to job listings and platform features, with longer commitments offering better per-month value.

The pricing structure is straightforward, with no tiered access levels or premium features locked behind higher price points. Every subscriber receives full access to all job listings, search filters, career resources, skills tests, and customer support regardless of which plan they choose. This egalitarian approach ensures that budget-conscious job seekers aren't disadvantaged compared to those who can afford annual subscriptions.

We Work Remotely offers a money-back guarantee, though the terms are somewhat restrictive. Members can request a refund within thirty days if they're unsatisfied with the service, but they must demonstrate that they've actively used the platform by applying to jobs and utilizing the available resources. This policy protects We Work Remotely from users who sign up, download job listings, and immediately request refunds without genuinely trying the service.

The subscription automatically renews unless canceled, which is standard practice for subscription services. The platform sends email reminders before renewal dates, giving members ample opportunity to cancel if they've found employment or no longer need the service. Cancellation is straightforward and can be completed through the account settings without needing to contact customer support.

When evaluating whether We Work Remotely is worth the cost, consider the time saved by avoiding scam listings and low-quality opportunities. If the subscription helps you find legitimate employment even one week faster than you would have using free job boards, the investment pays for itself. The pricing transparency and clear value proposition earned high marks in our verification assessment.
