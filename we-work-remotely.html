We Work Remotely Review

One of the largest and most established remote job boards globally.

4.5 / 5.0

Last Updated: January 21, 2026

Overview and First Impressions

We Work Remotely (WWR) is a veteran in the remote job space, having been around since 2011. It boasts a massive following and is often the first place companies go to post remote-only positions. The site is clean, straightforward, and focuses purely on job listings without the clutter of excessive articles or side features. Its reputation is built on volume and a strong presence in the tech sector, making it a reliable source for developers, designers, and customer support professionals.

While the sheer number of listings is impressive, job seekers should be prepared to filter through a high volume of applications, as competition can be fierce. The quality control is generally good, but it doesn't offer the same level of pre-vetting as some newer, more niche boards.

Pros and Cons

Pros

  • Largest remote job board by volume of listings.
  • Strong focus on tech and software development roles.
  • Clean, simple, and easy-to-navigate user interface.
  • Global reach with jobs from companies worldwide.
  • High-quality, remote-first companies frequently post here.

Cons

  • High competition for popular roles due to large user base.
  • Job posting fee is high ($299), which can deter smaller companies.
  • Less diversity in non-tech industries compared to general boards.
  • Filtering options are basic and could be more granular.
  • No built-in application tracking or profile features.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Is We Work Remotely free for job seekers?

Yes, We Work Remotely is completely free for job seekers. You can browse all listings, set up email alerts, and apply directly to jobs without any cost.

How much does it cost to post a job on We Work Remotely?

The standard job posting fee on We Work Remotely is $299 for 30 days. They also offer premium add-ons for increased visibility, such as a featured listing or social media promotion.

What types of remote jobs are most common on We Work Remotely?

We Work Remotely is known for its high-quality, professional roles, with a strong focus on tech and software development. You'll find many listings for programming, design, customer support, and marketing positions.

How does We Work Remotely compare to Remote Job Assistant?

We Work Remotely is a general remote job board with a strong focus on tech, while Remote Job Assistant is a curated platform that focuses on verified, high-paying remote roles across various industries. Both are excellent, but Remote Job Assistant is often preferred for its strict vetting process and focus on quality over quantity.
