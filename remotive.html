Remotive Review: Is It Worth It for Remote Jobs?

A deep dive into one of the most established remote job boards, specializing in tech and startup roles.

4.5 / 5

Last Updated: January 21, 2026

Our Verdict on Remotive

Remotive has established itself as one of the most reliable and long-standing remote job boards, particularly within the **tech and startup sectors** [2]. Founded in 2014, it has built a reputation for curating high-quality, fully remote positions, distinguishing itself from general job aggregators that often mix in hybrid or location-restricted roles. Our objective review for 2026 confirms that Remotive remains a top-tier resource for job seekers and a valuable platform for companies looking to hire remote talent.

The platform's strength lies in its focus. By concentrating on roles like software development, design, product management, and customer support for tech companies, it provides a highly relevant stream of opportunities for a specific audience. Users consistently praise its **clean, intuitive interface** and the quality of its email newsletter, which delivers a curated list of new openings directly to their inbox [4] [12].

However, this focus is also its main limitation. Job seekers outside the tech ecosystem—such as those in marketing, finance, or human resources for non-tech industries—may find the volume of relevant listings lower compared to broader boards. Furthermore, while the quality is generally high, some users have noted the occasional inclusion of intern or lower-value postings, which can dilute the experience slightly [1].

For recruiters, Remotive offers a straightforward pricing model, starting at $299 for a 30-day job post, with options for increased visibility. This premium pricing helps ensure that only serious companies post, contributing to the overall quality of the listings.

Remotive Pros and Cons

Pros

  • Highly curated and verified remote job listings.
  • Strong focus on the tech, software, and startup industries.
  • Clean, modern, and easy-to-navigate user interface.
  • Long-standing reputation and trusted brand in the remote work space.
  • Frequent updates with over 100 new jobs posted daily.

Cons

  • Primarily focused on tech; less suitable for non-tech roles.
  • Job posting price is premium, which may exclude smaller companies.
  • Occasional inclusion of intern or lower-value positions.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Is Remotive free for job seekers?

Yes, browsing the job board, viewing listings, and applying for jobs is completely free for job seekers. Remotive generates revenue primarily through job posting fees paid by companies.

What types of remote jobs are most common on Remotive?

Remotive specializes in remote jobs within the tech industry. The most common roles include Software Development, Engineering, Product Management, Design (UI/UX), and Customer Support for tech companies and startups.

How much does it cost for a company to post a job on Remotive?

As of early 2026, the base price for a standard job listing on Remotive is $299 for a 30-day posting. They also offer premium options for increased visibility and reach.

Is Remotive a legitimate job board?

Yes, Remotive is a highly legitimate and well-respected remote job board. It has been operating since 2014 and is known for its curated approach, which helps filter out scams and non-remote positions.
