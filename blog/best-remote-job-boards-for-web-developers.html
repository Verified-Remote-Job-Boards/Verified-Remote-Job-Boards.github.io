Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is the best remote job board for web developers?

The 'best' board depends on your experience level and niche. For high-quality, curated roles, **Remote Job Assistant** is highly recommended. For a broader range of listings, especially for entry-level to mid-level roles, platforms like We Work Remotely and RemoteOK are excellent starting points. Always check multiple boards to maximize your opportunities.

Are paid remote job boards worth the investment?

For experienced developers, yes. Paid boards often filter out low-quality or scam listings, providing access to higher-paying, more serious opportunities. They also tend to have fewer applicants per job, increasing your chances of getting noticed. Consider it an investment in your job search efficiency.

How can I tell if a remote job listing is legitimate?

Look for several red flags: generic email addresses (e.g., @gmail.com), requests for money or personal financial information upfront, vague job descriptions, and companies that are difficult to verify online. Legitimate boards like those listed here perform some level of vetting, but always cross-reference the company on LinkedIn and their official website.

Should I only apply to web developer-specific remote job boards?

While niche boards are great for targeted roles, don't ignore general remote job boards. Many companies post their developer roles on broader platforms to reach a wider audience. A balanced approach, combining niche and general boards, is usually the most effective strategy.
