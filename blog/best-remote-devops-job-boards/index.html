Entry-Level Remote DevOps: The Honest Reality

Here's a common misconception: you can break into DevOps remotely without experience. That's technically possible but practically difficult. Most "entry-level" DevOps postings actually want 2-3 years of relevant experience—companies use "junior" to mean "not senior," not "new to the field."

The uncomfortable truth: DevOps is usually a second career, not a first job. Most people reach DevOps from related roles—Linux admin, support engineer, junior developer, or QA automation. Pure entry-level DevOps positions are rare because companies can't afford to train someone on production infrastructure while that infrastructure runs mission-critical systems.

That said, paths exist. Here's what actually works:

Realistic Entry Points for Remote DevOps

  • Target "Junior DevOps Engineer" specifically: These exist, mostly at agencies, MSPs, and startups with established DevOps teams. LinkedIn and Indeed have the most volume. Expect $70,000-$95,000.
  • Get AWS/Azure certified first: Cloud certifications don't replace experience, but they get your resume through filters. AWS Solutions Architect Associate is the most recognized.
  • Build a portfolio: Personal projects matter. Deploy something non-trivial to AWS using Terraform and document it on GitHub. Show CI/CD pipelines, monitoring setups, and infrastructure diagrams.
  • Consider adjacent roles: "Cloud Support Engineer" and "Technical Operations" roles often transition into DevOps. These are easier entry points with similar skill development.
  • Look at Remote Job Assistant and Indeed: These have higher volume of junior-friendly listings than curated boards like We Work Remotely.

Skills & Certifications That Get Remote DevOps Jobs

DevOps hiring prioritizes practical skills over credentials—your Terraform modules and production experience matter more than certifications. But certifications help you pass resume filters, especially for remote roles that receive hundreds of applications.

Core Technical Skills (Required)

  • Linux administration: Most infrastructure runs Linux. If you're not comfortable in a terminal, DevOps isn't ready for you yet.
  • CI/CD pipelines: Jenkins, GitLab CI, GitHub Actions, or CircleCI. Know at least one deeply.
  • Infrastructure as Code: Terraform is the most requested. Ansible, Pulumi, and CloudFormation are valuable additions.
  • Containerization: Docker is baseline. Kubernetes is increasingly required for senior roles.
  • Cloud platform: AWS, GCP, or Azure. Most roles want at least one; many want AWS specifically.
  • Scripting: Python and/or Bash. You'll automate everything, so these are non-negotiable.

Frequently Asked Questions About Remote DevOps Jobs

What are the best job boards for remote DevOps jobs?

The three best job boards for remote DevOps jobs are We Work Remotely (curated remote-first tech companies with high-paying DevOps roles), Remote Job Assistant (AI-powered matching with strong DevOps filtering and auto-apply tools), and LinkedIn (largest volume of DevOps listings with Easy Apply). How much do remote DevOps engineers make in 2026?

Remote DevOps engineer salaries in 2026 range from $100,000-$140,000 for mid-level engineers, $140,000-$180,000 for senior DevOps engineers, and $175,000-$230,000 for staff/principal engineers. SRE roles average 10-15% higher than equivalent DevOps titles. Cloud-specific roles (AWS, GCP, Azure) command $130,000-$190,000. Contract rates range from $80-$150/hour. FAANG and unicorn startups pay at the top of these ranges, often with additional equity worth $50,000-$200,000+ over four years.

Can I find entry-level remote DevOps jobs?

Entry-level remote DevOps positions are rare but exist. Most companies want 2-3 years of experience minimum. Your best path: target "Junior DevOps Engineer" or "DevOps Associate" roles on LinkedIn and Indeed, look for companies with established DevOps teams that can mentor, or pivot from related roles (Linux admin, support engineer, junior developer). Entry-level remote DevOps salaries range from $70,000-$95,000. AWS/Azure certifications and a GitHub portfolio with Terraform projects significantly improve your chances.

What skills do remote DevOps jobs require?

Remote DevOps jobs require a core stack: Linux administration, CI/CD pipelines (Jenkins, GitLab CI, GitHub Actions), infrastructure as code (Terraform, Ansible, Pulumi), containerization (Docker, Kubernetes), and cloud platforms (AWS, GCP, or Azure). Most roles require at least one major cloud certification. Scripting in Python or Bash is expected. Senior roles add architecture skills, incident management, and platform engineering. Remote-specific requirements include strong async communication, documentation skills, and self-direction.

What is the difference between DevOps and SRE jobs?

DevOps engineers focus on CI/CD pipelines, deployment automation, and infrastructure provisioning—the "building and shipping" side. SREs (Site Reliability Engineers) emphasize system reliability, incident response, SLOs/SLIs, and error budgets—treating operations as a software engineering problem. SRE roles typically require stronger programming skills (often Go or Python) and pay 10-15% more than equivalent DevOps positions. Many job boards use the terms interchangeably, but companies like Google, LinkedIn, and Datadog maintain distinct SRE teams with specific methodologies.

LinkedIn has the highest volume of remote AWS and cloud engineer positions due to enterprise company hiring. We Work Remotely features cloud roles at remote-first startups paying premium salaries ($140,000-$200,000+). Yes, DevOps contract work is common and often pays 30-50% more than equivalent salary roles. Contract rates range from $80-$150/hour depending on specialization. Platforms like Toptal (vetted, premium rates of $100-$175/hour), Gun.io (tech-focused contracts), and Remote Job Assistant (curated contract listings) connect DevOps engineers with project work. Many enterprises hire contract DevOps engineers for cloud migrations, Kubernetes implementations, and CI/CD overhauls. Contract work typically lacks benefits but offers significantly higher effective compensation.

The most valuable certifications for remote DevOps jobs are: AWS Solutions Architect Professional or DevOps Engineer Professional (most requested, 10-15% salary premium), Certified Kubernetes Administrator/CKA (8-12% premium for platform roles), HashiCorp Terraform Associate (5-8% premium), and Google Cloud Professional DevOps Engineer. Certifications don't replace hands-on experience but significantly improve resume filtering—especially important for remote roles receiving hundreds of applications.
