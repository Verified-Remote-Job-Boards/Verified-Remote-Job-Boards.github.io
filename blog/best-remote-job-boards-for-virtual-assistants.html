Best Remote Job Boards for Virtual Assistants

Last Updated: January 21, 2026

Introduction

The demand for skilled Virtual Assistants (VAs) is soaring, and finding the right remote job board is the first critical step in a successful career. This guide cuts through the noise to present the best platforms for VAs, from specialized sites to general remote work hubs. We've analyzed each board based on job quality, ease of use, and, most importantly, authentic user reviews.

1. Remote Job Assistant (The VA Specialist)

Remote Job Assistant is a highly specialized platform dedicated exclusively to connecting Virtual Assistants with long-term, high-quality remote roles. It focuses on matching skills with company culture, making it a favorite among experienced VAs looking for stability and growth.

Pros:

  • Highly curated, VA-specific job listings.
  • Focus on long-term contracts and full-time employment.
  • Excellent support and resources for VAs.

Cons:

  • Fewer total listings compared to massive general boards.

2. FlexJobs (The Vetted Option)

FlexJobs is a subscription-based service that guarantees every job is legitimate and scam-free. They manually screen all listings, which is a huge time-saver for VAs tired of sifting through low-quality posts. They offer a wide range of remote, part-time, and flexible roles.

Pros:

  • 100% scam-free guarantee.
  • Excellent filtering options for specific VA skills.
  • Access to career resources and skills tests.

Cons:

  • Requires a paid subscription.
  • Some listings are not exclusive to FlexJobs.

3. Upwork (The Freelance Giant)

Upwork is the largest global platform for freelancers, offering countless short-term gigs and long-term contracts for VAs. While it requires VAs to actively bid on projects, the sheer volume of opportunities makes it a necessary stop for any remote professional.

Pros:

  • Massive volume of jobs, from quick tasks to long-term contracts.
  • Built-in payment protection and contract management.
  • Global reach.

Cons:

  • High service fees (up to 20%).
  • Intense competition and a race to the bottom on pricing.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Is a paid job board worth it for a Virtual Assistant?

For many VAs, yes. Paid boards like FlexJobs and Virtual Vocations vet their listings, saving you time and protecting you from scams. If you value quality over quantity and want to avoid the intense competition of free sites, a paid subscription can be a worthwhile investment that quickly pays for itself with a good job placement.

What is the difference between a general remote job board and a VA-specific one?

General remote job boards (like Remote.co or We Work Remotely) list jobs across all industries. VA-specific boards (like Remote Job Assistant) focus exclusively on administrative, technical, and creative assistant roles. The latter often provides more targeted resources and higher-quality leads for VAs.

How can I stand out on highly competitive platforms like Upwork?

To stand out on platforms like Upwork, focus on specialization. Instead of being a "general VA," market yourself as a "Social Media VA for E-commerce" or a "Technical VA for SaaS Startups." Create a strong, detailed profile, and write highly personalized proposals that clearly address the client's specific needs, rather than using generic templates.
