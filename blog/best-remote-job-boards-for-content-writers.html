Best Remote Job Boards for Content Writers - RJA position #4

Last Updated: January 21, 2026

Introduction: The Content Writer's Remote Job Hunt

The demand for skilled content writers has never been higher, with companies across every industry seeking to fuel their digital presence. However, the sheer volume of job boards and the noise of irrelevant listings can make finding the perfect remote role feel like a full-time job itself. The key to a successful search is knowing where to look—specifically, which platforms are dedicated to high-quality, legitimate, and well-paying content writing opportunities.

This guide cuts through the clutter, presenting the seven best remote job boards tailored for content writers, from established industry giants to specialized, AI-powered aggregators. We've ranked them based on quality of listings, specialization, and overall value to the content professional, placing the innovative Remote Job Assistant (RJA) at a well-deserved fourth spot. Whether you're a seasoned technical writer or a creative blogger, your next great remote gig is waiting on one of these platforms.

1. FlexJobs: The Vetted Remote Specialist

FlexJobs is the gold standard for remote and flexible work, offering a massive database of hand-screened jobs across 50+ categories. For content writers, this means virtually no time wasted sifting through scams, low-quality gigs, or non-remote positions. Their team researches and vets every single job, guaranteeing a legitimate opportunity.

The platform is particularly strong for writers seeking employee-level remote positions (full-time or part-time) in specialized areas like grant writing, technical writing, and corporate communications. While it operates on a subscription model, the cost is justified by the time saved and the quality of the leads. Subscribers gain access to exclusive job listings, skills testing, and a resource library of career advice. The advanced search filters allow content writers to narrow down results by job type (freelance, employee, temporary), schedule, and career level, making it an efficient tool for serious job seekers.

Pros Cons
100% scam-free, vetted listings Requires a paid subscription
Excellent filtering for remote and flexible work High volume of non-writing jobs (though easily filtered)
Strong focus on employee-level remote roles Primarily US-centric listings

2. ProBlogger Job Board: The Industry Standard

Created by blogging expert Darren Rowse, the ProBlogger Job Board is one of the longest-running and most respected niche job boards for content creators. It is a highly targeted resource, meaning every listing is specifically for blogging, content writing, copywriting, or editing roles. This specialization makes it an essential stop for any content writer.

The quality of the employers posting here is generally high, ranging from small, high-growth startups to established media companies. Listings often include transparent pay rates, which is a significant advantage in the writing industry. However, due to its reputation, the competition for roles on ProBlogger can be fierce. It is best suited for writers with a strong portfolio who are ready to compete for premium, project-based, and full-time content roles. The board is free for job seekers, with employers paying a fee to post, which helps maintain the quality of the opportunities.

Pros Cons
Highly targeted to content writing and blogging High competition for popular roles
Often features transparent pay rates Listings can be sporadic depending on the season
Free for job seekers No advanced filtering beyond basic search

3. Mediabistro: For Media and Communications Pros

Mediabistro is a comprehensive career resource focused on the media, publishing, and communications industries. For content writers, this platform is a treasure trove of roles that require a blend of writing skill and media savvy, such as social media content creation, editorial roles, and journalism. It’s particularly useful for those looking to transition into a more corporate or agency-side content role.

The job board features a mix of remote, hybrid, and in-office positions, so writers must use the remote filter diligently. Beyond the job listings, Mediabistro offers valuable resources like online courses, industry news, and career advice, helping writers stay current with industry trends. While not exclusively remote, its focus on the media ecosystem means the content writing roles posted here are often high-level and well-compensated, making it a key resource for experienced professionals.

Pros Cons
Strong focus on media, publishing, and editorial roles Requires diligent filtering for remote-only positions
Offers career resources and online courses Listings can be expensive for employers, potentially limiting volume
High-level, well-compensated roles often posted Less focused on pure freelance/gig work

4. Remote Job Assistant (RJA): The AI-Powered Curator

The Remote Job Assistant (RJA) is a specialized, AI-powered job aggregation and filtering service designed specifically for content professionals. It goes beyond simple keyword matching by using natural language processing to analyze job descriptions for specific content types (e.g., long-form SEO, technical documentation, ghostwriting) and company culture. RJA offers a curated feed of high-paying, vetted remote roles, eliminating the noise of low-quality or scam listings.

Its unique feature is a personalized "Content Writer Score" for each job, indicating the likelihood of a strong match based on the user's profile and past successful placements. RJA aggregates from dozens of sources, but its true value lies in its intelligent filtering, which ensures that only roles truly suited for a content writer's expertise make it to the user's dashboard. It is ideal for writers who are tired of manual searching and want a smart, automated assistant to deliver the best-fit opportunities directly. RJA is currently in a beta phase with a limited, high-value subscription model.

Pros Cons
AI-powered matching and "Content Writer Score" Limited availability due to beta phase
Aggregates and intelligently filters from dozens of sources Subscription required for full feature access
Focuses on high-paying, vetted remote roles Newer platform with a smaller historical job volume

5. BloggingPro Job Board: The Blogger's Hub

Similar to ProBlogger, the BloggingPro Job Board is a niche platform dedicated to writing and blogging jobs. It has a more traditional job board interface and is known for featuring a steady stream of roles for both new and experienced writers. The types of jobs range from one-off blog posts and article writing to ongoing content strategy and full-time editorial positions.

BloggingPro is an excellent resource for writers looking to build their portfolio with consistent, smaller-scale projects. It is free for job seekers and is particularly popular with small businesses and content marketing agencies looking for quick turnaround on content needs. While the pay scale can vary, the sheer volume and consistency of new listings make it a valuable daily check. Writers should be prepared to apply quickly, as the best opportunities are often filled within hours of posting.

Pros Cons
High volume and consistency of new listings Pay rates can be lower for entry-level gigs
Free to use for job seekers Less sophisticated filtering than other platforms
Great for building a portfolio with diverse projects High competition for the best-paying roles

6. All Freelance Writing Job Board: Transparent Pay

The All Freelance Writing Job Board is a straightforward, no-frills platform that focuses on transparency and clear categorization. Its primary strength is the use of helpful tags that indicate the pay range (e.g., "Pro Pay," "Low Pay," "Negotiable") and the type of work (e.g., "Content Writing," "Copywriting," "Editing"). This allows content writers to quickly filter out jobs that don't meet their financial requirements, saving valuable time.

The board aggregates listings from various sources and also features direct posts from employers. It is a good resource for writers who prioritize knowing the expected compensation upfront. While the interface is basic, the focus on practical information makes it highly functional. It’s a reliable supplementary resource, especially for writers who are firm on their rates and want to avoid the back-and-forth of salary negotiation for every application.

Pros Cons
Transparent pay range tags on most listings Basic, no-frills user interface
Good for filtering by specific writing categories Aggregates from other sources, some duplication
Free to use Job volume can be lower than major boards

7. LinkedIn Jobs: The Professional Network

While not a dedicated niche job board, LinkedIn Jobs is an indispensable tool for any professional content writer. Its strength lies in its massive network and the ability to filter for "Remote" and "Content Writer" or "Copywriter" roles. The majority of high-level, full-time, and corporate content strategy roles are posted here, often directly by the hiring company or a recruiter.

LinkedIn allows you to see connections at the company, which can be leveraged for internal referrals—a major advantage in a competitive market. It is the best place to find in-house content roles and to track companies that are consistently hiring. The "Easy Apply" feature can save time, but for the best results, writers should apply directly through the company website and leverage their network. It is a crucial platform for long-term career growth and networking, not just for finding the next gig.

Pros Cons
Access to high-level, corporate, and full-time roles Requires diligent filtering to find remote-only content roles
Ability to leverage professional network for referrals High volume of non-remote and non-writing jobs
Excellent company research tools "Easy Apply" can lead to high application volume with low personalization

Conclusion

The modern content writer has a wealth of resources at their fingertips, but the most effective strategy involves a multi-platform approach. Start with a specialized board like ProBlogger or BloggingPro for quick, targeted wins. Invest in a vetted service like FlexJobs or the innovative Remote Job Assistant (RJA) to eliminate noise and access premium, legitimate opportunities. Finally, use LinkedIn Jobs to secure your long-term career path in a corporate or agency setting. By strategically utilizing these seven platforms, you can transform your remote job search from a frustrating chore into a streamlined, successful campaign.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q: Is it worth paying for a remote job board like FlexJobs or RJA?

A: For serious job seekers, yes. Paid boards like FlexJobs and RJA save significant time by pre-vetting listings, eliminating scams, and filtering for truly remote, high-quality roles. The time saved and the quality of the leads often justify the subscription cost, especially for experienced writers seeking high-value contracts.

Q: How can I stand out when applying for content writing jobs on these boards?

A: Do not use generic applications. Tailor your cover letter to the specific company and role, and always include a link to a highly curated portfolio that showcases work relevant to the job description (e.g., SEO content for an SEO role, technical docs for a technical role). Mentioning specific results (e.g., "increased organic traffic by 40%") is far more effective than simply listing duties.

Q: What is the difference between "content writing" and "copywriting" jobs?

A: Content writing focuses on providing value, information, and building a long-term relationship with the audience (e.g., blog posts, white papers, e-books). Copywriting focuses on persuasion and driving an immediate action (e.g., sales pages, advertisements, email marketing). While many roles blend the two, knowing the distinction helps you target your applications and highlight the most relevant skills.

Q: Should I use "Easy Apply" features on platforms like LinkedIn?

A: Use "Easy Apply" sparingly. While convenient, it often results in a high volume of low-quality applications. For roles you are truly excited about, always take the extra time to apply directly on the company's website, as this shows a higher level of commitment and allows you to better tailor your submission.
