Our Rigorous Verification Process Explained

We believe finding a remote job should be exciting, not frustrating. Our multi-step verification process ensures that every job board and listing we feature is legitimate, active, and truly remote-friendly.

Last Updated: January 21, 2026

Why Verification Matters

The remote job market is booming, but so is the number of scams, outdated listings, and "remote" jobs that require you to live in a specific city. Our process is designed to cut through the noise, providing you with a curated list of job boards you can trust. We don't just check if a website exists; we dive deep into their practices, data integrity, and user experience.

The 5-Step Verification Protocol

Every job board must pass the following five critical checks to earn our "Verified" badge:

1. Data Integrity and Freshness Check

We use automated tools and manual checks to ensure the job board's listings are current. We look for signs of stale data, duplicate posts, and generic, non-specific job descriptions. A high percentage of recently posted, unique jobs is mandatory.

2. True Remote-First Vetting

This is the most critical step. We verify that the companies and jobs listed are genuinely remote, not "remote-local" or hybrid roles disguised as fully remote. We examine the board's policies on geographic restrictions and interview companies about their remote work culture.

3. Company and Employer Legitimacy

We cross-reference the employers listed on the board with public business registries, LinkedIn profiles, and news sources. This step weeds out shell companies, multi-level marketing schemes, and outright scams. We prioritize boards that feature established, reputable companies.

4. User Experience and Transparency

A great job board is easy to use. We evaluate the site's navigation, search functionality, and overall design. Crucially, we check for transparency regarding pricing (if any), data usage, and clear contact information for support.

5. Community Feedback and Reputation

We monitor public forums, social media, and direct user submissions to gauge the board's reputation. Consistent negative feedback, especially concerning scams or poor support, leads to immediate review and potential de-verification.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is the purpose of the verification process?

The verification process is designed to filter out scams, outdated listings, and non-truly-remote jobs. It ensures that the job boards and listings we feature are legitimate, active, and genuinely remote-friendly, saving job seekers time and frustration. We aim to provide a curated list of job boards you can trust.

How often are job boards re-verified?

Job boards are subject to continuous monitoring and a full re-verification audit every six months. If a board fails to meet our standards at any point, its "Verified" status is immediately revoked and the board is removed from our featured list.

What happens if a job board fails the verification?

If a job board fails any of the five critical checks, it is either given a short period to correct the issues or is immediately removed from our verified list, depending on the severity of the failure. For instance, any indication of scam activity results in immediate and permanent removal.

Does verification mean every single job on the board is guaranteed?

While our process rigorously vets the job board's practices and overall listing quality, we cannot guarantee every single listing. However, the verification ensures the board has strong internal controls to maintain high-quality, legitimate, and remote-first listings, significantly reducing your risk.
